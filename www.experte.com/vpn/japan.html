With a VPN, you'll be able to borrow a Japanese IP address, making it possible to access content from the Land of the Rising Sun. However, this isn't possible with every VPN provider, not least since services seem to have considerably more difficulty in Japan than they do in the UK or USA.

If you take a virtual trip to Japan using your VPN, you'll be able to watch local films and series.

In this article, we'll let you know which VPNs from our sample were best at digitally tunneling to Japan.

Why Do I Need a VPN for Japanese Content?

VPNs which have servers in Japan offer several advantages:

  • Stream content from abroad
    By connecting to a VPN server in Japan, you'll be able to bypass geoblocking and access content from streaming providers like Netflix, Disney+, or Amazon Prime Video that would otherwise only be available in Japan.

  • Use Japanese streaming services
    There are many services, like U-Next, that cater only to the Japanese market and aren't available abroad. A number of Japanese TV networks also offer On-Demand content online, which can only be accessed outside of Japan with a VPN. It can be difficult or even impossible to register for such a service without a Japanese telephone number or payment method.

  • Overcome YouTube blocks
    YouTube also limits some content to viewers from specific regions. With a VPN, you can get around these restrictions.

  • Dodge censorship
    In some countries, the Internet is heavily censored. For example, the People's Republic of China uses the Great Firewall to filter out large parts of the Internet like Google, Wikipedia, and Twitter. With a VPN, you'll be able to get back on the free Internet. Since connection quality depends on how distant you are from the VPN server, changing your digital location to Japan is great for those in northern or eastern China.

There are many reasons to use a VPN with Japanese servers. In our EXPERTE.com VPN comparison, we thoroughly tested 27 providers. Only one question remains: Which is the best for Japan?

Best VPN for Japanese Content

Performance, user-friendliness, and security were the three factors that mattered most in our head-to-head VPN comparisons. If looking at Japan, it's also important that the provider operates servers in the country (nearly every service from our sample did), and that it's possible to access streaming services like Netflix & Co.

For that reason, we evaluated VPNs on how well they performed in streaming content from Japanese Netflix, and below, will introduce you to our Top 3. These services worked during testing, however, VPN performances can vary from day to day. So, even if you just were able to stream Japanese Netflix content yesterday, you might be out of luck today. Just update the server list and try again in a few hours or the next day.

Best Free VPN for Japan

All of the VPNs from our Top 3 are premium. ProtonVPN is the only free solution that offers access to a Japanese server. The Swiss provider was our favorite among the freemium options since it doesn't impose any data limits. As such, you can surf with a VPN to your heart's content at no charge. Alongside Japan, you'll also be able to access servers in the US and the Netherlands.

ProtonVPN is the only service from our sample that offers a free Japanese server.

However, ProtonVPN's free version (and indeed, all free VPNs from our sample) do not support video streaming of content from abroad. With ProtonVPN, this didn't make much of a difference, as even in its premium version, Netflix sussed out our attempts to stream videos from their Japanese database.

Netflix caught on to our attempt to stream content from Japanese Netflix with ProtonVPN.

In case you just need a Japanese IP address, but don't want to pay for it, ProtonVPN is the best option.

Are VPNs Legal in Japan?

Yes: Both using a VPN in Japan, as well as outside of the country to connect to servers based there are completely legal. However, when you access otherwise blocked content with a VPN, you go against the streaming service's terms and conditions. For example, Netflix expressly prohibits its subscribers from using VPNs to circumvent country or region restrictions.

In the worst case, your account could be blocked or terminated, however, we're not aware of this ever happening as the result of VPN usage. From a legal perspective, you have nothing to worry about, while other consequences are unlikely.

Conclusion

VPNs make it possible to connect to servers in Japan, circumventing censorship and country restrictions on streaming platforms like Netflix. This allows you to watch films and series only available to the Japanese market. However, not every VPN from our sample can get around Netflix's control measures, which appear rather robust for Japan.

Surfshark, NordVPN, and Private Internet Access were up to the challenge and allowed us to stream content from Japanese Netflix. With solid-strong performance, they also have user-friendly apps. In case you only need a Japanese IP address for surfing or accessing static content and don't want to pay for it, you can take advantage of ProtonVPN's free version. Just remember that video streaming is not possible with this option.

FAQs

How can I get a Japanese IP address?
⊖

The easiest way to obtain a Japanese IP address is to use a VPN that has servers in Japan. Once you've connected to a Japanese server, you'll be assigned a Japanese IP address.

Are VPNs legal in Japan?
⊖

Yes, using a VPN and connecting to a VPN server there from outside the country are legal. However, you will go against your streaming provider's terms and conditions if you stream Japanese content that is only licensed for the Japanese market.

Which VPN is best for Japan?
⊖

Surfshark, NordVPN, and Private Internet Access did particularly well since they allowed us to stream content from Japanese Netflix. We cannot give any 100% guarantees though, since availability changes from day to day.

Are there free VPNs for Japan?
⊖

With ProtonVPN's free version, you can access a Japanese server and surf the Web without data limits. Video streaming is not possible with this option though.
